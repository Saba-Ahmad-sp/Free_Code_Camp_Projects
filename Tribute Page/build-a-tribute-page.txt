1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu 
1954: Graduated from Saint Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli with a degree in Physics 
1960: Graduated from Madras Institute of Technology with a degree in Aeronautical Engineering 
1969: Transferred from the DRDO to the ISRO 
1980: India launched its first satellite, Rohini, into near-earth orbit using the SLV-III, which Kalam developed 
1980s–1990s: Led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, which developed the AGNI and PRITHVI missiles 
1992–1999: Served as the Chief Scientific Advisor to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the DRDO 
1998: Served as the chief project coordinator for India's Pokhran II nuclear tests 
1999–2001: Served as the Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India 
2002–2007: Served as the 11th president of India 
2015: Passed away after collapsing during a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management in Shillong
